    🏆 Year-End Finale
    1st Weekend

    Tournaments are held on Saturday & Sunday of the first weekend of the month. Exact dates are announced in advance.

    Match Rules
    • Format: Best of 3 sets
    • Set scoring: First to 11 points wins the set
    • Deuce: At 10-10, play continues until one player leads by 2 points
    • Service: Service alternates every 2 points; at deuce (10-10), service alternates every point
    • Match win: First player to win 2 sets wins the match
    Quarterly Tournament Format
    Seeding

    Players are seeded based on their live ranking points. For the inaugural tournament, players were divided into 3 skill tiers (Advanced, Intermediate, Beginner) based on known ability. From the second tournament onwards, live ranking points determine the seedings.

    Group Stage

    Players are distributed into 4 groups, ensuring top-seeded players are spread across different groups. Each group plays a round-robin — every player faces every other player in the group.

    Knockout Stage

    The top 2 players from each group advance to the quarterfinals (8 players). The knockout stage follows a single-elimination bracket:

    Quarterfinals (8) → Semifinals (4) → Final (2) → Champion
    Year-End Finale Format
    Qualification
    • Direct entry: Top 6 players in the live rankings qualify automatically
    • Qualifiers: Players ranked 7th and below play qualifier round-robin matches (in 2 groups) to fill the remaining 2 spots
    Finale Format (8 Players)
    • 8 players divided into 2 groups (Group A & Group B)
    • Round-robin within each group
    • Top 2 from each group advance to semifinals
    • Semis: A1 vs B2, A2 vs B1
    • Winners play the Final
    Finale Points (Cumulative)

    Unlike quarterly tournaments, the finale awards points for each match won:

    Stage Points per Win
    Round-robin match win200
    Semifinal win400
    Final win500
    Maximum (undefeated champion)1,500
    Quarterly Points System

    Quarterly tournaments use a placement-based points system:

    Placement Points
    🥇 Winner500
    🥈 Runner-up300
    🥉 Semifinalist180
    Quarterfinalist90
    Participant45

    Points accumulate across all tournaments. The cumulative total determines a player's live ranking and seeding for future tournaments.

    Tiebreakers
    Live Rankings Tiebreaker

    When two or more players have equal cumulative points, the tiebreak order is:

    1. Higher number of match wins
    2. Fewer match losses
    Group Stage Tiebreaker

    When two or more players are tied on match wins in group standings:

    1. Head-to-head result — the player who won the direct match ranks higher
    2. Set win percentage — sets won ÷ total sets played
    3. Points win percentage — individual points scored ÷ total points played across all sets

    The same tiebreaker order applies to both quarterly tournaments and the year-end finale group stages.
